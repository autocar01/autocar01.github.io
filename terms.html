Car Rental Terms Explainer

Understand rental agreements before you sign

Car Rental Basics

Understanding the fundamentals of car rental agreements can save you money and prevent unexpected charges.

Rental Period & Reservations +

Reservation vs. Confirmation

A reservation isn't a guarantee. Rental agencies may overbook, meaning your car might not be available when you arrive. Always confirm your reservation 24-48 hours before pickup.

Rental Period Calculation

Rental periods are typically calculated in 24-hour periods from the pickup time. Returning a car even 30 minutes late may result in being charged for an extra day.

Pro Tip: If possible, choose a pickup time that gives you flexibility for returns. For example, if you pick up at 2 PM, you have until 2 PM on the return date to avoid late fees.
Driver Requirements +

Required Documents

In most locations, you'll need:

  • Valid driver's license
  • Credit card in the renter's name
  • Proof of insurance (in some cases)
  • International drivers permit (for international rentals)

Age Requirements

Most rental companies require drivers to be at least 21 years old. Drivers between 21-25 usually pay a "young driver surcharge" that can range from $15-$40 per day.

Additional Drivers

Anyone who will drive the rental car must be listed on the rental agreement. Additional drivers usually incur a daily fee of $10-$15, though some companies waive this for spouses or business partners.

Important: Allowing someone who isn't listed on the rental agreement to drive the vehicle may void your insurance coverage and result in additional fees if discovered.
Vehicle Inspection +

Before Driving Away

Always inspect the vehicle carefully before leaving the rental location. Look for:

  • Exterior damage (dents, scratches)
  • Interior condition
  • Tire condition
  • Working lights and signals
  • Fuel level (usually full)
  • Mileage reading

Documentation

Take photos of any pre-existing damage and ensure it's noted on the rental agreement. This protects you from being charged for damage you didn't cause.

Pro Tip: Use your smartphone to take photos or videos of the entire vehicle, including close-ups of any damage, before driving away. Make sure the date and time are recorded.
Return Policies +

Fuel Requirements

Most rentals use a "full-to-full" policy, meaning you receive the car with a full tank and must return it full. If you don't, you'll be charged for refueling at an inflated rate (often 2-3 times the local fuel price).

Early/Late Returns

Early returns: Most companies do not offer refunds for unused days if you return the car early.

Late returns: Being late may result in an extra day's charge or late fees.

Different Return Location

Returning a car to a different location than where you picked it up (one-way rental) typically incurs a "drop fee" that can range from $50 to several hundred dollars.

Important: Always get a receipt when returning your vehicle and verify that the fuel level and mileage are accurately recorded.

Understanding Rental Car Insurance

Rental car insurance options can be confusing. Here's what you need to know about the different coverage types.

Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) / Loss Damage Waiver (LDW) +

What it covers: This isn't technically insurance, but a waiver that relieves you of financial responsibility if the rental car is damaged or stolen.

Cost: Typically $15-$30 per day.

Important notes:

  • CDW/LDW may have exclusions for negligent behavior (driving while intoxicated, using the vehicle off-road, etc.)
  • May still include a deductible (amount you're responsible for)
  • Your personal auto insurance or credit card may already provide this coverage
Pro Tip: Check if your credit card provides rental car coverage before purchasing CDW/LDW. Many premium credit cards include this benefit when you use the card to pay for the rental.
Liability Insurance +

What it covers: Protects you if you injure someone or damage their property while driving the rental car.

Cost: Typically $10-$15 per day.

Important notes:

  • Rental companies are required to provide state minimum liability coverage
  • Supplemental liability insurance increases this coverage (often up to $1 million)
  • Your personal auto insurance policy typically extends liability coverage to rental cars
Important: State minimum liability limits are often insufficient to cover serious accidents. Consider supplemental coverage if your personal policy has low limits.
Personal Accident Insurance (PAI) +

What it covers: Medical expenses for you and passengers in the rental car in case of an accident.

Cost: Typically $5-$10 per day.

Important notes:

  • May be redundant if you have good health insurance
  • Often includes a death benefit
  • Coverage limits are typically low compared to personal health insurance
Pro Tip: PAI is often overpriced for the coverage provided. Your existing health insurance and/or travel insurance likely provides better coverage.
Personal Effects Coverage (PEC) +

What it covers: Theft of personal belongings from the rental car.

Cost: Typically $2-$5 per day.

Important notes:

  • Often has low coverage limits
  • Your homeowners or renters insurance may already cover your belongings while traveling
  • Typically includes a deductible
Important: Never leave valuables in a rental car. Most rental car break-ins are opportunistic thefts when valuable items are visible.

Hidden Fees to Watch For

Car rental companies are notorious for adding fees that can significantly increase your final bill. Here are the most common hidden charges to be aware of.

Airport Surcharges +

What it is: Additional fees charged for rentals from airport locations.

Typical cost: 10-25% of your total rental cost.

Airport surcharges include:

  • Concession Recovery Fee: Rental companies pay the airport for the privilege of operating there, and they pass this cost to you.
  • Facility Charge: For maintenance of rental car facilities at the airport.
  • Transportation Fee: For shuttle services between terminals and rental facilities.
Pro Tip: Consider renting from an off-airport location, especially for longer rentals. Many companies offer free pickup from your hotel or the airport to their off-airport locations.
Fuel Charges +

What it is: Charges for refueling if you don't return the car with a full tank.

Typical cost: 2-3 times the local fuel price per gallon/liter.

Fuel options typically include:

  • Full-to-Full: You get a full tank and must return it full (most economical).
  • Prepaid Fuel: You pay for a full tank upfront (usually at a competitive rate) and can return it empty.
  • Full-to-Empty: You pay for refueling at an inflated rate.
Important: The prepaid fuel option only makes sense if you're sure you'll return the car nearly empty. Otherwise, you're paying for fuel you don't use.
Mileage Restrictions +

What it is: Limits on how far you can drive without incurring additional charges.

Typical cost: $0.20-$0.40 per mile over the limit.

Mileage policies include:

  • Unlimited mileage: No restrictions (most common in the US)
  • Daily mileage cap: A limit per day (e.g., 150 miles/day)
  • Overall mileage cap: A limit for the entire rental period
Pro Tip: If planning a road trip, make sure your rental includes unlimited mileage. Otherwise, calculate your expected mileage carefully.
Additional Driver Fees +

What it is: Charges for having more than one person authorized to drive the rental car.

Typical cost: $10-$15 per additional driver per day.

Exemptions: Some companies waive this fee for:

  • Spouses or domestic partners
  • Business colleagues on corporate rentals
  • Members of their loyalty programs
Important: Never let someone drive who isn't on the rental agreement. This could void your insurance coverage and result in significant liability.
Early/Late Return Fees +

Early Return Fee: Some companies charge if you return the car before your scheduled return date.

Late Return Fee: Charged if you return the car after the scheduled return time.

Typical costs:

  • Early return: May result in a rate recalculation that removes any discounts for longer rentals
  • Late return: Hourly charges up to a full day's rate, plus potential inconvenience fees
Pro Tip: If you need to return a car early or expect to be late, call the rental company ahead of time. They may be able to adjust your reservation to avoid fees.
